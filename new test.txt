Ans 2:
Global warming is the gradual increase in the Earth’s average temperature due to the excessive emission of greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide (CO₂) and methane. These gases are released from human activities like burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes.

The rise in global temperatures leads to melting of polar ice caps, rising sea levels, and extreme weather conditions such as floods, droughts, and hurricanes. It also negatively affects biodiversity and ecosystems.

To control global warming, it is important to adopt renewable energy sources like solar and wind energy, reduce carbon emissions, and promote sustainable practices such as afforestation and energy conservation. Immediate action is necessary to protect the environment for future generations.
